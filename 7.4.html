7.4 – Doubles/Triples Air Brake Check

You must also make the following checks on your double or triple trailers.

7.4.1 – Additional Air Brake Checks

Check That Air Flows to All Trailers (Double and Triple Trailers)

  1. Secure the vehicle.

    Use the tractor parking brake and/or chock the wheels to hold the vehicle.

  2. Build normal air pressure.

    Wait for air pressure to reach normal, then push in the red “trailer air supply” knob.

  3. Charge emergency and service lines.

    This supplies air to the emergency (supply) lines. Use the trailer handbrake to provide air to the service line.

  4. Check emergency line at rear.

    Open the emergency line shut‑off valve at the rear of the last trailer. Air should escape, showing the system is charged. Close the valve afterward.

  5. Check service line at rear.

    Open the service line valve to confirm service pressure reaches all trailers (service brake or handbrake must be applied). Close the valve afterward.

  6. Verify air to the back.

    If you do not hear air escaping from both lines, check that all shut‑off valves on trailers and dollies are OPEN. Air must reach the back for brakes to work.

Test Tractor Protection Valve

  1. Charge trailer air brake system.

    Build normal air pressure and push the “air supply” knob in.

  2. Shut off engine.

    Turn off the engine.

  3. Fan off air pressure.

    Step on and off the brake pedal several times to reduce air pressure.

  4. Watch trailer air supply control.

    The control should pop out (normal → emergency) when pressure drops to the manufacturer’s range (usually 20–45 psi).

  5. Understand the risk if it fails.

    If the valve fails, a hose or trailer leak could drain all tractor air, causing emergency brakes to activate and risking loss of control.

Test Trailer Emergency Brakes

  1. Charge trailer air brake system.

    Charge the system and ensure the trailer rolls freely.

  2. Apply trailer emergency brakes.

    Pull out the trailer air supply control (tractor protection valve control) or place it in the “emergency” position.

  3. Verify brakes hold.

    Pull gently with the tractor to confirm the trailer emergency brakes are engaged.
